Papers

A Tiny Convolutional Neural Network Driven by System Identification for Vibration Anomaly Detection at the Extreme Edge

In this study, the professors Federica Zonzini and Luca De Marchi, my colleagues Letizia Burioli and Andi Gashi, and I explored an approach for detecting anomalies in sensor vibration diagnostics. We combine the benefits of embedded System Identification (eSysId) as a data compression technique with the energy advantages of Machine Learning (TinyML). By conducting experiments on a dataset for Structural Health Monitoring (SHM), we have achieved classification scores (above 90%) in assessing the health of the well-known Z24 bridge. The inference time is minimal, taking less than 44 ms. It consumes very little power when running on three different general-purpose microprocessors. This makes it an exciting solution for developing the next generation of Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS) focused on SHM. We strongly believe that our work presents opportunities in the field of ITS. It allows us to extract damage features directly at the edge without transmitting long-time series data over the monitoring network. We cover topics such, as AI, Machine Learning, TinyML, Anomaly Detection Vibration Diagnostics, Structural Health Monitoring and Intelligent Transportation Systems.
